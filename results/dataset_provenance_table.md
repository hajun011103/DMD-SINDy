# Dataset Provenance and Content Summary

| Dataset | Source / Link | Real or Simulated? | How It Was Derived | What It Contains | Reliability | Main Caveats |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Kutz Cylinder (vorticity) | Brunton-Kutz companion repo: <https://github.com/dynamicslab/databook_matlab>  Data bundle: <http://databookuw.com/DATA.zip>  Expected local files: `data/benchmarks/kutz_cylinder/CYLINDER_ALL.mat`, `data/benchmarks/kutz_cylinder/CYLINDER_basis.mat` | Simulated CFD benchmark, not experimental | Full 2D cylinder-wake flow snapshots were generated on a structured grid, then post-processed into reduced-order ingredients such as mean field, reference unsteady field, and modal basis. In the local files this appears as `UALL`, `VALL`, `VORTALL`, plus `vortAVG`, `vortUNSTEADY`, and `vortPHI`. | Structured-grid velocity and vorticity snapshots. In the local copy: `UALL`, `VALL`, `VORTALL` each have shape `(89351, 151)` with `nx = 199`, `ny = 449`. The basis file also contains `vortPHI` with 41 modes and mean/shift-reference fields. | High for reduced-order-model and modal-benchmark studies because it is curated, clean, and directly aligned with mean-field wake modeling. | The exact original CFD solver pedigree is not clearly documented in the public companion repo. It is less representative of noisy real measurements because it is a processed benchmark dataset with built-in modal structure. |
| DeepXDE / Nektar Cylinder (u,v) | PINNs project page: <https://maziarraissi.github.io/PINNs/>  Original file: <https://github.com/maziarraissi/PINNs/blob/master/main/Data/cylinder_nektar_wake.mat>  Nektar++ background: <https://www.nektar.info/>  Expected local file: `data/benchmarks/deepxde_cylinder/cylinder_nektar_wake.mat` | Simulated CFD benchmark, not experimental | A high-fidelity incompressible cylinder-wake simulation at `Re = 100` was sampled in space and time over a wake-region window. The PINNs page describes the data as spatio-temporal training data in a rectangular region of the cylinder wake. The local `.mat` file stores scattered-point samples of velocity and pressure over time. | Scattered wake-region coordinates, time samples, velocity, and pressure. In the local copy: `X_star (5000, 2)`, `t (200, 1)`, `U_star (5000, 2, 200)`, `p_star (5000, 200)`. | High for CFD-based system identification and PINN/ROM benchmarking because it is a widely reused public benchmark with clear variable definitions and published usage context. | It is still a clean numerical dataset rather than field measurements. Spatial coverage is a sampled wake region, not the full domain, so reduced models inferred from it may be less globally constrained over long-time rollouts. |

## Practical Interpretation

| Question | Kutz Cylinder | DeepXDE / Nektar Cylinder |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Best viewed as | ROM-oriented modal benchmark | Scattered CFD observation benchmark |
| Native variables used in our benchmark | Vorticity snapshots | Velocity snapshots `(u, v)` |
| Extra structure available | Mean field, reference unsteady field, modal basis | Coordinates, time, velocity, pressure |
| Why it is useful here | Tests whether a method can recover classic wake mean-field / shift-mode structure | Tests whether a method can learn from sampled wake data with less built-in ROM structure |
| Why long-term modeling is hard | The shift-mode dynamics are small but physically important | The sampled-window dynamics are easier short-term but still drift long-term |
